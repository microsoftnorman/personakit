## Segment dynamics

Mid-market buyers (50-500 employees) increasingly bypass enterprise sales
motions entirely. 71% of evaluated mid-market PM-tool purchases in our
synthetic dataset closed self-serve within 30 days of trial start; 8%
required a sales conversation; the remainder churned out before trial end.

## Pain points (top 5, by mention frequency in synthetic interview corpus)

1. **Tool sprawl** — average mid-market PM team uses 6.4 tools that hold
   the same task data.
2. **Onboarding cost** — average time-to-first-value for a new PM tool was
   measured at 11.4 days; teams above that threshold churned at 3.1× the rate.
3. **Status drift** — 64% of teams reported their PM tool's status was
   "out of date by Tuesday".
4. **Pricing friction at user 50** — the seat-count bracket where 78% of
   self-serve teams renegotiate or churn.
5. **Reporting export** — 42% of teams export to spreadsheets weekly because
   the in-tool reporting does not match leadership's expected format.

## Willingness-to-pay signals

- Buyers consistently rejected per-seat pricing above $25/seat for non-AI
  features.
- AI-assisted planning features clear $5-10/seat add-on willingness in 38%
  of evaluated buyers.
- Bundling AI into the Business tier (rather than charging separately)
  correlated with 1.6× higher attach.

## Competitive positioning notes

- Asana wins on familiarity but loses on Jira-replacement deals.
- Linear wins on engineering-team adoption but is rejected by ops/marketing
  buyers as "too dev-coded".
- ClickUp wins on feature breadth but loses on perceived complexity.
- monday.com wins on dashboards but loses on developer integrations.
