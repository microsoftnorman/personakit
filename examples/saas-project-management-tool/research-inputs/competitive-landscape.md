# Competitive landscape — mid-market PM SaaS (FICTIONAL summary)

> ⚠️ Fabricated for Personakit's reference example.

## Asana

- **Wedge:** Familiarity. Buyers default to Asana when the evaluator has
  used it before.
- **Weakness:** Loses Jira-replacement deals. Engineering teams reject it
  as "ticket-heavy without ticket-tooling".
- **Pricing posture:** $13.49/seat/mo Premium, $30.49/seat/mo Business
  (representative public rates).
- **Roadmap signals:** AI summaries shipped Q4 2025; auto-scheduling
  rumored for Q3 2026 launch.

## ClickUp

- **Wedge:** Feature breadth at low base price.
- **Weakness:** Perceived complexity. Loses to Tessera in segments where
  the buyer is the user.
- **Pricing posture:** $7-19/seat/mo across tiers.
- **Roadmap signals:** AI agents announced for 2026; pricing migration
  expected.

## Linear

- **Wedge:** Engineering-team adoption.
- **Weakness:** Rejected by ops/marketing as "too dev-coded". Does not
  compete in mid-market PMO deals.
- **Pricing posture:** $8-14/seat/mo.
- **Roadmap signals:** Roadmap planning module mid-2026.

## monday.com

- **Wedge:** Dashboards and visual workflows.
- **Weakness:** Developer integrations are perceived as shallow.
- **Pricing posture:** $9-19/seat/mo across tiers.
- **Roadmap signals:** AI agent suite Q2 2026.

## Implications for Tessera

- The "AI in Business tier" pricing posture is contested ground in 2026.
  Asana and ClickUp moving in the same direction.
- The "one status update propagates everywhere" wedge is uncontested
  among the four named competitors as of this snapshot.
- Engineering-team adoption is a Linear stronghold; Tessera should not
  fight there directly.
